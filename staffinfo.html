Ray Holder
MANAGER OF EMPLOYMENT SERVICES

Ray joined the STAR Center in 2001 after a 22-year career in the Navy. In his time working at the STAR Center as Manager of Employment Services, he has helped over 1100 people with disabilities join or rejoin the workforce.

Tara Janssen
Clarcksville, Middle TN

Tara graduated with a Master’s Degree in Occupational Therapy from Utica College in New York in 2009. Prior to joining STAR Center in January of 2020, Tara worked at Fort Drum rehabilitating Army soldiers who had sustained traumatic brain injuries. Her passions lie with serving veterans, working with those with physical impairments, and integrating technology into the lives of farmers with disabilities through STAR’s partnership with the TN AgrAbility Project.

Rich Henderson
Knoxville, East TN

Rich graduated with a Master's Degree from Milligan University in 2013 and has since served as an Occupational Therapist OTR/L helping individuals regain their independence. After joining the STAR Center in October of 2021, Rich states that he has been exposed to a whole new world in helping others with disabilities through assistive technology and adaptive aids, and that it is his calling.

Meredith Bailey
Cookeville, Middle/East TN

Meredith graduated with a master’s degree from Western Michigan University in 2017 and is a Certified Vision Rehabilitation Therapist (CVRT) and a Certified Instructional Specialist for the Visually Impaired (CATIS). She has experience working with individuals who have low vision, blindness, Deaf-blind, Deaf and Hard of Hearing. Her passion is working with individuals to discover their independence through the use of technology.

Marya Bailey

Marya has been a music therapist since 2014. She earned her bachelor's degree from Union University, attended Saint Mary-of-the-Woods College for music therapy training, and completed her internship at STAR. Marya’s favorite part about being a music therapist is the privilege of connecting with others through music and helping them to reach their full potential!

Lizzie Harris
Chattanooga, Southeast TN

Lizzie earned her degree in Audiology and Speech Pathology from the University of Tennessee at Knoxville and worked as an SLP-A in Oregon for 2 years after graduating. About Us

We help people with disabilities realize their potential. Serving all 95 Counties in Tennessee.In 1988, Chuck and Margaret Doumitt realized the need for disability resources after two of their children were diagnosed with Batten's Disease, thus founding The STAR Center. We serve others in the areas of education, employment, and independence.
